# Collage Styles — Design Characteristics Reference

A reference guide for 20 collage styles, covering core visual traits for use in art direction, prompt writing, and graphic design.

---

## 1. Ransom Note

- Letterforms assembled from mismatched newspaper and magazine clippings
- Varied font sizes and weights within a single word or line
- Inconsistent baseline — letters sit at different heights
- Raw scissor-cut edges, no clean trimming
- Mix of yellowed newsprint and glossy magazine surface
- Black and white fragments alongside color clippings
- High contrast, chaotic, and unsettling energy

---

## 2. Torn Paper

- Rough fibrous torn edges — no clean cuts
- Layered paper scraps building up form and depth
- Visible paper grain and pulp texture
- Soft drop shadows beneath each overlapping layer
- Warm neutral tones — cream, ivory, off-white
- Handmade tactile quality with organic imperfection
- Depth created through layering, not perspective

---

## 3. Textured Fill Lettering

- Large bold display letters acting as windows into layered imagery
- Interior of letterform filled with maps, botanicals, vintage ads, or newsprint
- Strong letter silhouette contrasted against clean background
- Rich interior texture vs. minimal exterior negative space
- Letterform as frame, not just type
- Works best with thick serif or display letterforms

---

## 4. Found Object

- Letters and shapes physically constructed from everyday objects
- Materials include matchsticks, cutlery, thread, twigs, tape, coins, pins
- Flat lay arrangement photographed from above
- Strong natural shadows cast from physical depth of objects
- Tactile and playful, inventive visual problem-solving
- Neutral surface background — white, concrete, wood grain

---

## 5. Magazine Cutout

- Bold glossy color blocks cut from luxury and lifestyle magazines
- High contrast clashing color combinations
- Photographic skin, fabric, and product textures within shapes
- Clean, precise cut edges
- Pop art energy — punchy, commercial, immediate
- Vibrant fluorescent and primary color palette
- Flat composition with no depth or shadow

---

## 6. Vintage Ephemera

- Layered postage stamps, ticket stubs, old receipts, handwritten labels
- Telegram fragments, aged paper currency, wax seal impressions
- Warm sepia, faded ink, and muted ochre tones
- Visible aging — foxing spots, fold creases, rubber stamp marks
- Overlapping paper fragments creating dense surface
- Nostalgic documentary atmosphere
- Aged paper or linen texture as background

---

## 7. Anatomical Collage

- Victorian medical and botanical engraving illustrations
- Detailed cross-section diagrams and scientific plate imagery
- Latin text annotation fragments as visual texture
- High contrast black engraving lines on aged cream paper
- Clinical precision meets artistic or surreal juxtaposition
- Numbered labels and measurement indicators
- Antique scientific illustration quality throughout

---

## 8. Surrealist Collage

- Dreamlike impossible scenes assembled from unrelated imagery
- Scale distortion — oversized objects in domestic or natural settings
- Victorian engraving sources mixed with photographic cutouts
- Uncanny calm atmosphere despite impossible content
- Seamless blending of unrelated visual worlds
- Influenced by Max Ernst, Hannah Höch visual language
- Eerie narrative ambiguity — neither fully logical nor fully abstract

---

## 9. Zine / Punk Collage

- Xerox photocopy distortion and image degradation
- High contrast black and white with harsh grain and noise
- Aggressive cropping with no regard for visual comfort
- Overlapping type and image with no clear visual hierarchy
- Raw DIY energy — hand-stapled and folded aesthetic feel
- Safety pin, tape, and correction fluid as design motifs
- Underground, anti-establishment, anti-polish attitude

---

## 10. Photo Montage

- Multiple photographic fragments composited into a unified scene
- Matched lighting and perspective across source images
- Cinematic realism — feels like a single photograph
- Smooth, invisible seams between image sources
- Narrative tension created by combining unrelated photographic worlds
- Hyperrealistic impossible scene with no visible collage seams
- Scale and color correction applied for seamless result

---

## 11. Decoupage

- Ornate overlapping decorative paper cutouts
- Dense floral, botanical, and figurative motifs
- Rich jewel tones — emerald, ruby, sapphire — with gold accents
- Lacquered glossy surface finish implied throughout
- Victorian parlor and cabinet-of-curiosities aesthetic
- Intricate repetitive patterning with no visible background
- Every surface covered — no negative space

---

## 12. Botanical Pressed

- Dried and pressed flowers, leaves, ferns, seed pods
- Translucent petal texture with light passing through
- Natural color fading — dusty greens, muted pinks, dried yellows and browns
- Delicate and fragile quality — specimens feel preserved and fragile
- Deliberate scientific arrangement — herbarium specimen aesthetic
- Clean cream, white, or pale grey background
- Minimal, quiet, contemplative composition

---

## 13. Map Collage

- Vintage cartographic fragments used as texture and form
- Topographic contour lines, city grids, compass roses, sea charts
- Aged cream, sepia, and faded ink tones
- Geographic text annotations, place names, and coordinates as visual texture
- Navigation and exploration atmosphere
- Antique cartography aesthetic — hand-drawn lines, aged paper
- Layered map fragments creating abstract surface pattern

---

## 14. Fashion Editorial Collage

- Luxury fashion magazine fragments as primary material
- High gloss fabric shots, runway photography, product close-ups
- Bold masthead typography and perfume ad color palettes
- Aspirational, sleek, high-status energy
- High contrast against clean white negative space
- Couture aesthetic — expensive, deliberate, desirable
- Glossy surface quality throughout all elements

---

## 15. Data / Infographic Collage

- Charts, graphs, technical diagrams, and statistical tables as visual texture
- Clinical grid structures and data visualization fragments
- Monochrome or tightly limited color palette
- Intellectual and analytical atmosphere
- Data used decoratively — form over function
- Technical document and annual report aesthetic
- Numbers, percentages, and axis labels as typographic texture

---

## 16. Letterpress + Collage

- Aged ink-pressed typefaces combined with handcut collage elements
- Deep impression texture visible on thick paper stock
- Ink spread and bleed at letter edges
- Warm off-white or cream paper with earthy ink tones
- Visible debossing from physical press impression
- Artisanal print shop and workshop atmosphere
- Mix of typographic rigidity and hand-collaged spontaneity

---

## 17. Fabric & Textile Collage

- Woven fabric swatches, lace fragments, embroidery thread
- Stitching lines and knit textures assembled into composition
- Tactile material depth — layers feel physically thick
- Mixed pattern language — plaid, floral, solid cloth combined
- Visible thread ends at cut edges
- Soft warm material color palette — natural fiber tones
- Handcrafted domestic aesthetic

---

## 18. Cyanotype Collage

- Blueprint Prussian blue photographic print aesthetic
- White silhouettes of objects and botanicals on deep blue ground
- Layered cyanotype exposures creating tonal depth
- Sun-print and photogram quality throughout
- High contrast — deep blue and white only, no other colors
- Scientific and poetic simultaneously
- UV light and chemistry implied in surface texture

---

## 19. Double Exposure Collage

- Two or more images merged with transparent ghosting overlap
- Landscape, forest, or texture bleeding through portrait silhouette
- Soft luminous blending at merge zones
- Cinematic and ethereal mood
- Duotone or monochrome palette preferred
- Dreamlike photographic composite quality
- Misregistration and soft transparency as intentional aesthetic

---

## 20. Japanese Chiyogami

- Traditional patterned washi paper cutouts in precise arrangements
- Bold repetitive motifs — cranes, waves, cherry blossoms, chrysanthemums
- Flat graphic color with no shading or dimensional form
- Rich jewel tones and gold accents on deep backgrounds
- Precise geometric or organic cutting — no rough edges
- Refined, ceremonial, and deliberate atmosphere
- Japanese woodblock print and kimono textile aesthetic
