# Vibes — Design Characteristics Reference

A reference guide for 30 distinct visual vibes, covering atmospheric, emotional, and aesthetic language for use in poster design, art direction, and prompt writing.

---

## MOOD & EMOTION

### 1. Melancholic
- Desaturated muted palette — grey-blue, dusty mauve, faded olive, washed out cream
- Low contrast, soft edges, no harsh lines or sharp transitions
- Large areas of empty negative space — composition feels lonely and sparse
- Diffused indirect light with no strong shadows or highlights
- Heavy visual weight at the bottom — compositions feel sunken or settled
- Slow visual rhythm — wide spacing between elements, no urgency
- Texture of aged, worn, or water-damaged surfaces
- Rain, fog, bare trees, empty rooms as recurring visual motifs

### 2. Euphoric
- Saturated vibrant palette — electric yellow, hot pink, vivid cyan, bright orange
- High contrast with luminous light blooms and glowing edges
- Dynamic diagonal composition — everything feels in motion upward
- Dense visual energy — composition full, no dead space
- Radial or explosive element arrangement from central point
- Strong rhythmic repetition suggesting pulse and beat
- Lens flare, light streaks, and bokeh as visual texture
- Figures or forms with arms raised, open, expansive gestures

### 3. Nostalgic
- Warm faded tones — sepia, amber, dusty rose, faded yellow, cream
- Soft grain and film texture overlaid across entire surface
- Slightly overexposed or washed-out quality — memories feel bright but imprecise
- Rounded soft edges — nothing sharp or clinical
- Analog imperfection — light leaks, chromatic aberration, vignette at edges
- Everyday domestic and childhood motifs — suburban streets, old toys, family scenes
- Serif or hand-lettered typography with slightly worn ink
- Faded vintage print quality — colors slightly shifted from original

### 4. Uncanny
- Hyper-realistic detail applied to something slightly wrong
- Normal domestic or familiar environment with one impossible element
- Flat even lighting — no dramatic shadows, everything equally visible
- Muted naturalistic palette with one jarring out-of-place color
- Symmetry pushed too far — feels artificial and unsettling
- Scale distortion — familiar objects at wrong sizes
- Stillness and silence implied — no movement, no life, no warmth
- Faces or figures with something subtly off — proportion, expression, placement

### 5. Romantic
- Warm soft palette — blush pink, dusty rose, warm ivory, gold, soft peach
- Diffused soft focus light — edges glow, nothing sharp
- Organic flowing forms — curves, drapes, petals, fabric folds
- Lush floral and botanical motifs throughout
- Gentle visual rhythm — composition breathes and moves slowly
- Soft golden or warm backlight creating halo effects
- Texture of velvet, silk, petal, and aged paper
- Intimate scale — feels close, personal, and whispered

### 6. Rebellious
- High contrast black and white with aggressive accent color — red, yellow, or raw orange
- Rough DIY texture — xerox grain, torn edges, spray paint, marker strokes
- Broken grid — elements placed in defiance of alignment and order
- Aggressive typography — condensed, distorted, oversized, overlapping
- Collaged fragments suggesting destruction and reconstruction
- Visual noise and distortion as intentional aesthetic choice
- Anti-polish — imperfection and rawness are the point
- Confrontational composition — elements push toward the viewer

## ATMOSPHERIC

### 7. Golden Hour
- Warm amber, deep gold, burnt orange, and long soft shadow palette
- Low horizontal light raking across surfaces creating dramatic warmth
- Long shadows stretching across ground planes
- Backlit subjects with glowing rim light at edges
- Soft lens flare and light bloom in composition
- Silhouettes against warm luminous sky
- Everything bathed in amber — cool colors entirely suppressed
- Hazy atmospheric softness at horizon and background

### 8. Midnight
- Deep near-black palette — navy, charcoal, deep indigo, black
- Isolated pools of cold artificial light against total darkness
- High contrast between lit and unlit zones — no gradual transition
- Moon, stars, or single light source as compositional anchor
- Cool blue-grey highlights on surfaces caught by ambient night light
- Silence and stillness implied — sparse, slow, empty
- Reflections in wet surfaces — doubling and distorting light sources
- Urban or natural environment stripped of daytime color and life

### 9. Neon Night
- Deep black or near-black background absorbing all ambient light
- Saturated neon colors — electric pink, cyan, purple, acid green
- Strong colored light bloom and glow spreading from neon sources
- Wet reflective surfaces doubling and smearing neon colors
- Chromatic aberration and light bleed at high-contrast edges
- Fog or rain diffusing neon glow into soft atmospheric halos
- Urban nightscape — signs, windows, puddles, rain-slicked streets
- High contrast between neon-lit and shadow zones

### 10. Foggy
- Desaturated cool palette — grey, pale blue, soft white, muted green
- Forms dissolve and lose detail as they recede into mist
- Atmospheric perspective pushed to extreme — background nearly invisible
- Soft diffused light with no visible source or direction
- Foreground elements sharp, everything behind increasingly ghosted
- Quiet, muffled, and isolated atmosphere
- Texture of moisture — damp surfaces, condensation, soft wet air
- Horizon line obscured or entirely consumed by mist

### 11. Candlelight
- Warm intimate palette — deep amber, burnt orange, warm brown, soft gold
- Single small light source creating dramatic chiaroscuro
- Sharp warm highlights surrounded by deep rich shadow
- Flickering implied — soft uneven light distribution
- Intimate small scale — feels close, private, and enclosed
- Texture of wax, wood, stone, and aged fabric
- Faces and hands emerge from darkness into warm pool of light
- Edges of composition fall into near-total darkness

### 12. Harsh Noon
- Bleached high-key palette — stark white, pale grey, washed-out color
- Direct overhead light creating minimal shadow — forms flatten
- Hard-edged shadows directly beneath objects — short and intense
- Overexposed quality — details in highlight zones burned out
- Heat and aridity implied — dry, stark, unforgiving environment
- No atmospheric haze — everything sharp and mercilessly clear
- Colors desaturated by intensity of light — bleached and raw
- Uncomfortable exposure — nowhere to hide, everything visible

## CULTURAL & ERA

### 13. Vaporwave
- Pastel palette — soft pink, lilac, mint, baby blue, warm peach
- Retro 1980s–90s computer graphic and consumer aesthetic
- Greek statue fragments, Roman busts, classical architecture as motifs
- Grid lines, sunset gradients, and palm trees as recurring elements
- Glitch distortion and scan line texture overlaid on surface
- Early 3D render quality — low polygon geometry, flat shading
- Japanese katakana and Latin typography mixed together
- Nostalgic digital consumer utopia atmosphere — shopping malls, cassette tapes

### 14. Retrofuturism
- Chrome metallic surfaces, gleaming white, primary accent colors
- Mid-century modern optimism about technology and the future
- Atomic age motifs — starbursts, orbiting electrons, rocket ships
- Bold geometric sans-serif typography with futuristic confidence
- Illustrated rather than photographic — clean vector or airbrush quality
- Space age color palette — silver, orange, turquoise, cream, black
- Streamlined aerodynamic forms — everything curved and efficient
- World's Fair and space program visual language throughout

### 15. Y2K
- High-gloss metallic and iridescent surfaces — chrome, holographic, silver
- Translucent plastic and liquid gel textures
- Hyper-saturated digital palette — cyan, magenta, electric blue, lime
- Early CGI render quality — bubble letters, inflated 3D forms
- Futuristic optimism mixed with digital glitch anxiety
- Pixel fonts, matrix-style text, and early web interface aesthetics
- Alien, technological, and post-human visual motifs
- Plastic fantastic consumer culture energy — shiny, disposable, exciting

### 16. Dark Academia
- Deep rich palette — burgundy, forest green, navy, charcoal, aged ivory
- Old European university and library aesthetic
- Classical architecture, candlelit reading rooms, ancient books as motifs
- Warm but dim lighting — gaslight and incandescent quality
- Dense typographic texture — serif fonts, handwritten annotations, Latin text
- Aged paper, leather, wood, and brass material textures
- Intellectual melancholy — beauty found in studying, solitude, and autumn
- Romantic pursuit of knowledge as visual subject

### 17. Cottagecore
- Soft natural palette — sage green, warm cream, dusty rose, butter yellow, clay
- Lush hand-illustrated botanicals — wildflowers, herbs, mushrooms, berries
- Handmade and domestic aesthetic — embroidery, woven fabric, pottery
- Soft diffused natural daylight — overcast or dappled through leaves
- Abundance of natural textures — linen, moss, wood grain, dried flowers
- Slow and gentle visual rhythm — nothing urgent or sharp
- Pastoral rural setting — meadows, cottage gardens, forest clearings
- Nostalgic pre-industrial domestic life as ideal

### 18. Cyberpunk
- Dark desaturated base — near-black, deep grey, wet concrete
- Aggressive neon accent colors — acid green, electric blue, hot magenta
- High-density layered composition — urban visual noise and complexity
- Corporate dystopia motifs — surveillance cameras, holograms, advertising
- Rain-slicked reflective surfaces doubling neon signage
- Biomechanical and technological augmentation imagery
- Fragmented overlapping text — multiple languages, code, glitch
- Tension between extreme wealth and extreme poverty in same frame

## AESTHETIC MOVEMENTS

### 19. Wabi-Sabi
- Muted natural palette — ash grey, clay, moss, weathered wood, pale sand
- Imperfect irregular forms — nothing precisely symmetrical or machine-made
- Texture of aging — rust, crack, erosion, patina, worn surface
- Asymmetric restrained composition with generous negative space
- Single humble subject given full quiet attention
- No decorative excess — only what is necessary remains
- Beauty found in incompleteness, transience, and natural decay
- Slow meditative visual rhythm — composition breathes and rests

### 20. Maximalism
- Saturated rich palette — multiple competing color families coexisting
- Every surface covered — no empty negative space allowed
- Layered patterns, textures, motifs, and ornament simultaneously active
- Dense visual complexity rewarding extended looking
- Eclectic mix of references — historical, cultural, and stylistic
- No single focal point — eye moves freely through dense visual field
- Abundance, excess, and opulence as explicit visual values
- Controlled chaos — underlying order beneath surface complexity

### 21. Minimalism
- Monochrome or severely restricted palette — one or two colors maximum
- Single subject or element given entire compositional space
- Extreme negative space — emptiness as primary design material
- No decorative detail — every element must justify its presence
- Precise geometric forms with mathematically clean edges
- Sans-serif typography at considered scale and placement
- Silence and restraint as the loudest possible statement
- Luxury implied through what is removed not added

### 22. Brutalist
- Raw concrete, exposed structure, and unfinished surface textures
- Heavy monolithic forms with no decorative softening
- High contrast black and white with occasional aggressive accent
- Typography large, bold, condensed, and unapologetically dominant
- Grid broken deliberately — asymmetric and structurally confrontational
- Industrial materials and construction process made visible
- No attempt to beautify or conceal underlying structure
- Power, permanence, and institutional weight as visual atmosphere

### 23. Ethereal
- Pale luminous palette — soft white, pearl, pale gold, translucent lavender
- Soft focus and diffused light — no hard edges anywhere
- Transparent overlapping layers creating gossamer depth
- Delicate botanical, celestial, and atmospheric motifs
- Composition feels weightless — elements float rather than sit
- Fine intricate detail at small scale — delicate rather than bold
- Light appears to emanate from within surfaces not from outside
- Spiritual, transcendent, and otherworldly atmosphere throughout

### 24. Sublime
- Vast scale contrast — tiny human or object against enormous landscape
- Dramatic natural forces — storm, cliff, ocean, mountain, void
- Dark brooding palette — deep grey, charcoal, storm green, slate blue
- Overwhelming compositional weight — viewer feels small and awed
- Low horizon placing vast sky or void as dominant element
- Turner and Friedrich atmospheric drama and scale
- Awe, terror, and beauty experienced simultaneously
- Nature as force beyond human comprehension or control

## SENSORY & TACTILE

### 25. Warm & Dusty
- Muted warm palette — ochre, terracotta, sand, faded rust, warm grey
- Fine grain dust texture overlaid across entire surface
- Soft unfocused edges — heat haze quality blurring transitions
- Faded quality — colors appear sun-bleached and time-worn
- Dry arid material textures — cracked earth, aged wood, worn fabric
- Slow heavy visual rhythm — composition feels tired and unhurried
- Strong horizontal emphasis — flat landscapes, long low horizons
- Memory of heat, dryness, and long slow afternoons

### 26. Cold & Clinical
- Cool desaturated palette — stark white, pale grey, steel blue, ice
- Hard precise edges — everything sharp and unambiguous
- Flat even lighting with no warm tones anywhere
- Smooth reflective surfaces — glass, metal, polished concrete
- Maximum negative space — sterile and controlled emptiness
- Sans-serif typography at precise mechanical scale
- No organic forms — geometry only, nothing irregular or handmade
- Efficiency, precision, and emotional distance as atmosphere

### 27. Lush & Overgrown
- Deep saturated natural palette — forest green, deep emerald, wet earth, humid shadow
- Dense layered botanical forms filling entire composition
- Overlapping leaves, vines, roots, and flowers competing for space
- Dappled filtered light breaking through canopy overhead
- High humidity implied — surfaces glisten, air feels thick and alive
- No architectural or geometric forms — nature has consumed everything
- Rich visual complexity rewarding close examination
- Abundance, wildness, and untamed natural growth as atmosphere

## NARRATIVE ATMOSPHERE

### 28. Mythic
- Rich deep palette — gold, deep red, midnight blue, burnished bronze
- Monumental heroic scale — figures larger than life against vast backdrops
- Classical architectural ruins, celestial phenomena, sacred landscapes
- Strong directional light — divine or supernatural light source implied
- Symbolic motifs — serpents, eagles, fire, water, celestial bodies
- Timeless atmosphere — no identifiable historical period
- Visual weight and gravitas — everything feels permanent and significant
- Oral tradition and legend implied — story larger than any single moment

### 29. Dreamlike
- Shifting soft palette — colors bleed into each other without hard boundaries
- Impossible spatial logic — scale, gravity, and perspective rules broken
- Soft focus throughout — nothing fully sharp or resolved
- Familiar objects in unfamiliar combinations or contexts
- Slow drifting visual rhythm — composition feels unanchored
- Transparent ghosted layers overlapping without clear depth order
- Warm and cool tones coexisting without natural light source logic
- Narrative implied but never resolved — meaning always just out of reach

### 30. Apocalyptic
- Desaturated ash palette — grey, burnt ochre, smoke black, rust, dead white
- Destroyed or decaying architectural forms — ruins, rubble, collapse
- Dramatic broken light — harsh shafts through smoke, ash, or cloud
- Extreme scale — vast destruction dwarfing any human presence
- Dense atmospheric particle texture — smoke, ash, dust filling air
- Silence implied — vast emptiness where life once was
- Horizon obscured by smoke, fire glow, or total grey overcast
- Beauty and terror coexisting in the wreckage of the familiar
