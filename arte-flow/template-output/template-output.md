Example 1 - Catalog pick (no placeholders)
Create a collage artwork with:
- Artstyle: Bauhaus  
  Use a Bauhaus-inspired visual language emphasizing functional design, geometric abstraction, and bold simplicity. Focus on clean shapes, minimal decoration, and strong visual balance between form and color.

- Collage approach: modular-collage-grid  
  Arrange elements in a structured modular system where each piece fits into a grid-based unit. The collage should feel engineered and systematic, with repeated visual blocks creating rhythm.

- Composition: Swiss grid layout  
  Apply a strict Swiss-style grid to align all elements precisely. Maintain strong typographic hierarchy and consistent spacing to ensure clarity and order.

- Character pose: The Crossed Arms  
  Position the subject with arms crossed to communicate confidence and defiance. The posture should feel grounded and assertive, reinforcing the rebellious tone.

- Vibe: Rebellious  
  Convey a sense of resistance and bold attitude through sharp contrasts and striking visual tension. The overall mood should feel unapologetic and energetic.

- Subject/theme: Underground music flyer with abstract instruments and motion shapes  
  Design the collage as a music flyer featuring stylized instrument forms and dynamic shapes suggesting sound and movement. Keep the theme gritty yet structured, blending abstraction with musical identity.

- Text in collage:  
  "NOISE NIGHT" at top center, "LIVE 10 PM" at bottom right  
  Ensure the title is dominant and immediately readable, while the event time acts as a secondary anchor point in the layout.

Additional direction:
Use primary red, yellow, and blue along with black and white for a classic Bauhaus palette. Keep edges sharp with no gradients, emphasize geometric repetition, and establish a strong hierarchy between text and shapes.
