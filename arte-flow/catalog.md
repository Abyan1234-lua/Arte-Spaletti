# Catalog Options (Reference-Based)

Use these when the user chooses **Pick from catalog list**.
All options below are sourced from:
- `references/artstyle-references.md`
- `references/composition-layout.md`
- `references/vibes.md`
- `refereces/collage-styles.md`

When asking users to pick, present each group with numbered ranges so users can answer with numbers.

## Artstyles (from `artstyle-references.md`, range 1-15)
- `1. art-nouveau`: Ornamental organic curves, botanical motifs, and gilded decorative elegance.
- `2. bauhaus`: Strict geometric grid, primary colors, and functional no-ornament clarity.
- `3. italian-renaissance-classicism`: Balanced classical architecture with warm fresco tones and ideal proportion.
- `4. cubism`: Fragmented geometric planes and multi-viewpoint angular deconstruction.
- `5. fauvism`: Wild non-naturalistic color and bold expressive flat brush energy.
- `6. futurism`: Aggressive diagonals, speed lines, and industrial motion-focused dynamism.
- `7. magical-realism`: Realistic worlds with one impossible element rendered as ordinary.
- `8. impressionism`: Loose light-driven brushwork with soft atmospheric edges.
- `9. neoclassicism`: Severe symmetry, cool restraint, and monumental civic seriousness.
- `10. pop-art`: Flat high-contrast commercial imagery with halftone print aesthetics.
- `11. post-impressionism`: Structured expressive strokes and symbolic emotional color.
- `12. primitivism`: Simplified flat forms, strong outlines, and ritual folk pattern energy.
- `13. rococo`: Pastel ornamental abundance with playful theatrical aristocratic softness.
- `14. suprematism`: Pure geometric abstraction on white space with radical reduction.
- `15. ukiyo-e`: Flat bold color, precise outlines, and poetic asymmetric woodblock character.

## Collage (derived from `collage-styles.md`, range 1-20)
- 1. `ransom-note-collage`: Mismatched cutout letterforms with irregular alignment, mixed print sources, and chaotic high-contrast energy.
- `2. torn-paper-layering`: Layered torn edges with visible paper grain, soft shadows, and tactile handmade depth.
- `3. textured-fill-lettering`: Bold letterforms filled with detailed textures like maps, botanicals, or print fragments, contrasted against clean space.
- `4. found-object-collage`: Letters and shapes constructed from real-world objects arranged in flat lay with natural shadows and physical depth.
- `5. magazine-cutout-collage`: Clean-cut glossy magazine fragments with vibrant colors, sharp edges, and bold pop-style contrast.
- `6. vintage-ephemera-collage`: Layered aged paper artifacts like stamps and tickets with sepia tones and nostalgic worn textures.
- `7. anatomical-collage`: Scientific engravings and diagram fragments arranged with high-detail linework and antique medical illustration aesthetics.
- `8. surrealist-collage`: Dreamlike compositions with scale distortion and unexpected juxtapositions creating uncanny narrative scenes.
- `9. zine-punk-collage`: Raw photocopy textures with harsh contrast, aggressive cropping, and chaotic DIY underground energy.
- `10. photo-fragment-montage`: Seamlessly blended photographic fragments forming a unified, cinematic, hyperreal composition.
- `11. decoupage-maximalist`: Dense overlapping decorative cutouts with rich colors and no negative space, creating a fully covered surface.
- `12. botanical-pressed-collage`: Delicate arrangement of dried plant elements with soft natural tones and minimal, specimen-like composition.
- `13. map-collage`: Layered cartographic fragments with contour lines, place names, and aged navigation textures.
- `14. fashion-editorial-collage`: High-gloss fashion imagery combined with bold typography and clean, aspirational layout.
- `15. data-infographic-collage`: Charts, graphs, and technical diagrams used as structured visual texture in a clean analytical style.
- `16. letterpress-collage`: Ink-pressed typography with visible texture and imperfections combined with hand-cut collage elements.
- `17. fabric-textile-collage`: Layered fabric, stitching, and woven textures creating soft, tactile, handcrafted compositions.
- `18. cyanotype-collage`: Deep blue and white photogram-style imagery with high contrast and scientific poetic tone.
- `19. double-exposure-collage`: Overlapping images blended with transparency and soft transitions for an ethereal cinematic effect.
- `20. japanese-chiyogami-collage`: Precise patterned washi paper cutouts with flat color, repetition, and refined ornamental balance.

## Composition (from `composition-layout.md`, range 1-50)
- `1. manuscript-grid`: Single large content block with wide margins creating a calm, restrained, document-like layout.
- `2. column-grid`: Vertical column divisions structuring content flow with strong editorial rhythm.
- `3. modular-grid`: Row-column matrix organizing elements into consistent structured modules.
- `4. hierarchical-grid`: Flexible grid with varied column and row sizes based on content importance.
- `5. baseline-grid`: Consistent horizontal rhythm aligning text and elements across the layout.
- `6. compound-grid`: Multiple grid systems overlaid to create complex and layered structure.
- `7. typographic-led-layout`: Composition dominated by typography as the primary visual element.
- `8. image-led-layout`: Composition dominated by a single strong image with minimal text support.
- `9. centered-layout`: Symmetrical center-focused composition with formal and stable balance.
- `10. asymmetric-layout`: Intentionally imbalanced layout with visually resolved weight distribution.
- `11. split-layout`: Two contrasting zones dividing the composition horizontally or vertically.
- `12. layered-layout`: Overlapping elements arranged in depth planes for richness and complexity.
- `13. minimal-layout`: Extremely reduced composition using large negative space and few elements.
- `14. swiss-grid-layout`: Strict typographic grid with disciplined alignment and minimal styling.
- `15. collage-layout`: Freeform overlapping elements without rigid grid, expressive and dense.
- `16. full-bleed-layout`: Visual elements extend to all edges with no margins or framing.
- `17. rule-of-thirds`: Key elements placed along thirds intersections for dynamic balance.
- `18. golden-ratio`: Proportional layout based on 1:1.618 for natural visual harmony.
- `19. golden-spiral`: Spiral-based composition guiding the eye toward a focal point.
- `20. symmetry-layout`: Mirrored balance across an axis creating formal stability.
- `21. radial-composition`: Elements radiate from or converge toward a central focal point.
- `22. diagonal-composition`: Elements arranged along diagonal axes to create motion and tension.
- `22. framing-composition`: Foreground elements used to frame and direct focus to the subject.
- `23. negative-space-composition`: Empty space used actively to define and emphasize subject.
- `24. repetition-pattern`: Repeated elements creating rhythm, texture, and visual continuity.
- `25. contrast-tension`: Opposing visual elements creating hierarchy and dynamic energy.
- `26. top-down-hierarchy`: Visual flow starts at the top and moves downward through content.
- `27. bottom-anchored`: Primary visual weight sits at the bottom creating grounded stability.
- `28. central-focal`: Single dominant element centered with all others supporting it.
- `29. edge-tension`: Key elements pushed toward edges creating cropped dynamic framing.
- `30. z-pattern-flow`: Eye movement follows a Z-shaped scanning path across the layout.
- `31. f-pattern-flow`: Eye scans horizontally then vertically, suited for text-heavy compositions.
- `32. circular-flow`: Elements arranged to guide the eye in a continuous loop.
- `33. flat-composition`: No depth illusion, all elements exist on a single visual plane.
- `34. layered-depth`: Foreground, midground, and background separation creating spatial depth.
- `35. atmospheric-depth`: Depth created through fading color, contrast, and detail over distance.
- `36. scale-contrast-depth`: Depth implied through size differences between elements.
- `37. overlapping-depth`: Elements layered over each other to establish spatial hierarchy.
- `38. three-plane-depth`: Clear foreground, midground, and background zones for cinematic structure.
- `39. header-body-footer`: Layout divided into top, middle, and bottom content zones.
- `40. type-image-ratio`: Defined balance between typography and imagery shaping visual dominance.
- `41. anchor-point-layout`: Key elements placed at natural visual resting points for clarity.
- `42. symmetrical-balance`: Equal visual weight distributed across a central axis.
- `43. asymmetrical-balance`: Unequal elements balanced through visual weight and positioning.
- `44. radial-balance`: Visual weight distributed evenly around a central point.
- `45. crystallographic-balance`: Uniform distribution with no focal point, creating all-over pattern.
- `46. leading-lines`: Visual lines guiding the viewer’s eye toward focal areas.
- `47. eye-movement-path`: Designed sequence controlling how the viewer navigates the composition.
- `48. rhythm-repetition`: Repeated elements creating pacing and visual movement.
- `49. visual-tension`: Strategic placement of opposing forces to create energy.
- `50. visual-rest`: Areas of low density providing pause and balance within the composition.

## Vibes (from `vibes.md`, range 1-30)
- `1. melancholic`: Desaturated sparse mood with soft edges and lonely negative space.
- `2. euphoric`: Explosive saturated color, upward motion, and high-energy rhythm.
- `3. nostalgic`: Warm faded memory tones with grain, leaks, and analog softness.
- `4. uncanny`: Familiar scenes with subtle wrongness that feels unsettling.
- `5. romantic`: Soft warm florals, gentle curves, and intimate glowing light.
- `6. rebellious`: High-contrast raw DIY grit with broken-grid confrontational attitude.
- `7. golden-hour`: Amber low-angle light, long shadows, and warm cinematic haze.
- `8. midnight`: Deep near-black stillness with isolated cold light pockets.
- `9. neon-night`: Dark urban glow, neon bloom, and wet reflective surfaces.
- `10. foggy`: Mist-softened forms, muted cool tones, and muffled atmosphere.
- `11. candlelight`: Intimate warm chiaroscuro with strong shadow and soft flicker.
- `12. harsh-noon`: Bleached overhead light with stark exposure and minimal shelter.
- `13. vaporwave`: Pastel retro-digital nostalgia with grids, statues, and glitch texture.
- `14. retrofuturism`: Mid-century space-age optimism with chrome and aerodynamic forms.
- `15. y2k`: Glossy chrome-plastic futurism with early CGI digital intensity.
- `16. dark-academia`: Scholarly melancholy with deep tones, books, and old interiors.
- `17. cottagecore`: Gentle pastoral natural softness with handmade domestic warmth.
- `18. cyberpunk`: Neon dystopia, dense urban layers, and techno-social tension.
- `19. wabi-sabi`: Imperfect aged simplicity with muted natural restraint.
- `20. maximalism`: Pattern-rich visual abundance with controlled dense excess.
- `21. minimalism`: Severe reduction, strong silence, and high-value negative space.
- `22. brutalist`: Raw heavy structural force with bold uncompromising contrast.
- `23. ethereal`: Luminous weightless delicacy with translucent soft-focus layering.
- `24. sublime`: Vast awe-terror scale with dramatic natural power.
- `25. warm-and-dusty`: Sun-faded earthy heat with dry textured stillness.
- `26. cold-and-clinical`: Sterile cool precision with hard edges and emotional distance.
- `27. lush-and-overgrown`: Dense humid botanical abundance and untamed growth.
- `28. mythic`: Monumental symbolic legend tone with sacred dramatic lighting.
- `29. dreamlike`: Soft surreal drift with impossible logic and unresolved narrative.
- `30. apocalyptic`: Ash-toned ruin atmosphere with broken light and vast aftermath.
